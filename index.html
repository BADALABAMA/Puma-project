the suede became a classic while going through several names. beginning as the puma crack,the shoe evolved into the clyde, then puma states and finally globally adopted the name puma suede.

Puma

A first step in a long history driven by a speed and perfomance: The brothers Rudolf and Adolf Dassler founded the company "Grebruder Dassler Schuhfabrik" (Dassler Brothers Shoe Factory) in their hometown Herzogenaurach, Gremany. The brothers started their factory in their parents'home in 1919, moving into its proper facility in 1924. 28 years after founding their company, the Dassler brothers fell out and went separate ways. Rudolf Dassler moved inro another building, which belonged to the family. Together witch 14 employees, he transformed this storage facility into a factory and founded his own company. On October 1, 1948 "PUMA" was registered at the German Patent and Trademark Office.

Crack

Prior to 1968, the world had yet to adopt wearing sneakers fo leisure, or better yet, collecting for their own preasure. That meant shoes were purpose built and PUMA shoes we trainign shoes only. However, the world was changing, and PUMA's first "lifestyle" shoes the "CRACK" which was launched in 1968 prior to the Olympics in Mexico. Word "CRACK" was used to describe the best of its kind, for example the best player of a sport. To be called the "CRACK" of one's team was the highest possible award and, by transferring this connotation onto the shoe, one was perfectly able to highlight the actuality and superiority of the shoe.

1968 summer olympics

That same year, at the Summer Olympics, Tommie Smith became the first person to run the 200 metre sprint in under 20 seconds- and he did it wearing PUMA CRACK. At the medal ceremony, Tommie approached the podium with bronze-medalist John Carlos. Both raised their fist, in a what the athlete later described as "a human rights salute."This was a cruicial moment in sport, a moment than paved the way for future athletes to use their stage to stand up and speak out.
